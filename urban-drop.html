02 The core mechanic

How it works

A four-stage flow, run on the same calendar week after week. Predictable enough to memorize, tight enough to scale.

01 / Pre-order

Customer Pre-Orders

Orders close 48 hours before drop. No walk-ups. No impulse buys. Every unit is already sold.

02 / Prepare

Farm Prepares

Harvest, pull, pack, stage. Only what's been paid for leaves the farm.

03 / Load

Packed & Loaded

Vehicle loaded with confirmed orders. No surplus, no waste, no guesswork.

04 / Drop

Urban Pickup Node

Fixed location, fixed day, fixed time. Same every week. Customer shows up, picks up, done.

Customers don't browse. They pre-order and show up. That's what makes this scalable.
— The whole model in one sentence

05 Demand profile

Who we're selling to

Three distinct buyer types — each with a different reason to show up, but all converging on the same urban drop point.

Aspiring Homesteaders

  • Want chickens but don't know where to start
  • Interested in rabbits but haven't pulled the trigger
  • Want a "farm connection" without relocating
  • High curiosity, high LTV once trust is built

Existing Hobby Homesteaders

  • Need new bloodlines — rabbits, chickens
  • Looking for desert-adapted genetics
  • Want reliable compost & soil inputs
  • Already spending — need a better local source

Experience-Driven Buyers

  • Parents buying animals as pets
  • People who buy the story, not just the product
  • Less price-sensitive
  • Strong word-of-mouth multiplier — they talk
06 Operating discipline

Non-negotiable: pre-sale only.

If this rule slips, the model breaks. The whole structure runs on certainty before pack-out.

Do Not

  • Bring random inventory and hope it sells
  • Adjust pricing at the pickup window
  • Accept walk-up purchases
  • Offer "whatever's left over"

Do

  • Sell first → harvest → deliver
  • Close orders 48 hours before every drop
  • Require prepayment — no exceptions
  • Keep SKUs tight early (5–8 items max)
What this eliminates waste·guesswork·cash flow gaps·inconsistent experience

08 Failure modes

What kills this — and how we don't let it.

Four real ways this model breaks. Each has a known mitigation. None of these are theoretical.

01
Inconsistency
FailureSkip one week, customers disappear.
MitigationStart with one drop per week. Don't scale until 6–8 stable weeks are in the bag.
02
Overcomplication
FailureToo many SKUs too early overwhelms operations and customers.
MitigationCap at 5–8 items for the first two months. Add complexity only against demand signal.
03
No-Show Buyers
FailureShowing up with animals nobody picks up is a welfare and cost problem.
MitigationPrepayment required, no exceptions. No prepay, no reservation.
04
Animal Transport Stress
FailureHeat, handling, and distance are real factors in southern Arizona.
MitigationProper containment, shade, water. Short handling windows. Pre-load staging.
09 High-ROI start plan

How we launch

Validate before pack-out. Pre-sell before harvest. Stay small until the calendar is consistent.

Week 1–2 · Validate Interest
Prove demand before building anything

Simple landing page or Google Form: "Tucson Farm Drop — Interest List."

Share in Tucson homesteading groups, Facebook, NextDoor.

Week 3–4 · Pre-Sell First Drop
Real money, small menu

First-drop menu: laying hens + 1 rabbit listing + 1 mixed nursery box.

5–8 SKUs maximum. Prepayment required to reserve.

Month 2 · Run 2–4 Drops
Document what's pulling weight

Track demand patterns and best sellers.

Note which SKUs generate the most questions — those become the next consulting upsell.

Month 3+ · Expand Carefully
Layer in margin and recurrence

Add nursery items and soil products.

Introduce event-based drops.

Begin a subscription egg share for predictable recurring revenue.
