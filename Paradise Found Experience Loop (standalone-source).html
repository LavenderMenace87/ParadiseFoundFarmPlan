Core Business Logic Shift

From four parallel offerings — to one Living Systems Experience Loop

A single integrated loop where animals, water, soil, and plants form a visible regenerative cycle that directly increases plant sales and visitor spending.

Current Structure

  • Petting zoo experience
  • Nursery plant sales

  • General agritourism visits

Upgraded Structure
One integrated Living Systems Experience Loop
  • Animals → emotional hook + family anchor
  • Pond + floating gardens → signature ecosystem story
  • Compost transparency → trust in plant quality
  • Bees + BSF → biological proof systems
  • Nursery → conversion zone with establishment narrative

Both reinforce nursery trust and increase plant sales conversion without altering the core business model.

Stage 03.5 · Living Conversion Systems Node
The biological proof systems
A brief stop between compost and nursery. Visitors don't get a lecture — they see two living systems that quietly answer the questions they were already asking.
Pollination Layer · Bees
External validation
"Is this ecosystem healthy?"
  • Pollination engine for nursery, orchard, and herb garden
  • Honey + beeswax production stream — premium local product
  • Visible hives near orchard, herb garden, pollinator strips
  • Beeswax soaps + candles tie directly into the herb garden narrative
  • Healthy bees = subconscious validation of plant quality
Proof the ecosystem is stable and alive — not framed to visitors as "honey production."
Bioconversion Layer · Black Soldier Flies
Internal efficiency
"Is this farm actually self-contained?"
  • Converts manure + organic waste into high-protein larvae (animal feed)
  • Produces frass — a premium fertilizer for the nursery soil line
  • Accelerates compost throughput; cuts hidden feed costs
  • Optional viewing window: larvae bins, chickens / rabbits feeding on larvae
  • Links the manure stream to the future duck system feed loop
A natural nutrient recycler turning farm outputs into feed and fertilizer — not framed to visitors as "insects processing waste."

Systems as Revenue Amplifiers

Not a construction proposal — a revenue amplification layer

Each system below is compared against a traditional contractor build-out and an integrated on-site model. The difference is not just cost — it is whether the system becomes a recurring expense or a recurring revenue source.

This plan is not a construction proposal. It is a farm revenue amplification layer that increases per-visitor spend, increases nursery product value, reduces external input costs, converts existing waste streams into sellable inputs, and strengthens "authentic regenerative farm" pricing power.
